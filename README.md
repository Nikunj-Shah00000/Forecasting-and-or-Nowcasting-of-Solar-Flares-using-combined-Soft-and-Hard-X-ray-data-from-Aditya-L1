# 🌌 Mission Overview

Solar flares are among the most energetic explosions in our Solar System.

These events release enormous amounts of electromagnetic radiation that can affect:

🛰️ Satellites

📡 Communication Systems

🌍 Navigation Networks

⚡ Power Grids

🚀 Space Missions

To mitigate these risks, **ISRO's Aditya-L1 Mission** continuously observes the Sun from the **Lagrange Point L1**, providing uninterrupted measurements of:

### ☀️ SoLEXS

Solar Low Energy X-ray Spectrometer

### ☀️ HEL1OS

High Energy L1 Orbiting X-ray Spectrometer

SuryaDrishti-AI combines these observations with cutting-edge Artificial Intelligence to:

✅ Detect solar flares in real time

✅ Predict future flares before they occur

✅ Generate alerts for space-weather events

✅ Support satellite and mission safety

# 🌞 Understanding Solar Flares

A flare generally follows:

```text
Magnetic Energy Build-Up
            │
            ▼
 Soft X-Ray Enhancement
            │
            ▼
 Hard X-Ray Burst
            │
            ▼
 Flare Peak
            │
            ▼
 Decay Phase
```

The challenge is identifying subtle precursor signatures before the eruption.
